Gates of Hormuz

A real-time strait-authority game for the open web

About the Project

Gates of Hormuz puts you in charge of a major oil shipping lane. You're the Strait Authority: every vessel that wants through has to come past your desk first. It's a lightly fictionalized take on a real chokepoint — the tension of Papers, Please wrapped around a tycoon economy.

How It Works

Ships approach the gate in waves. For each one you make a call: clear it through, pull it aside to inspect, or divert it away. Clear a clean ship and you get paid. Wave a dirty one through, or stop traffic you shouldn't have, and it costs you. You start with three lives — run out and the run ends.

The hook is the inspection. Catch a ship carrying something it shouldn't and the seizure pays out big: the whole screen freezes for a beat, gold bursts, and the win actually lands before play resumes.

The Build

This is the first real-time game I've built for the open web. React 19 and Vite handle the shell, but the game itself runs on a hand-written canvas loop — ships, waves, economy, and the moment-to-moment feel all live in a single tick. Getting it onto CrazyGames and Poki meant building to each portal's SDK and constraints: save systems, ad timing, pause behavior, mobile and desktop, all spec'd to what each platform requires.

What I Learned

Feel is everything in a real-time game, and feel lives in details you can't plan on a whiteboard. The seizure payout did nothing until I added the freeze-frame and the gold burst — same mechanic, completely different game. Almost all the work after "it functions" was tuning that gap between working and good.

Status: In review — CrazyGames & Poki
